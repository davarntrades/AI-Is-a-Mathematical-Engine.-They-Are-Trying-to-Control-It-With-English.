## What Each Word Names

These four words are doing precise work. They are not interchangeable. Each one names a different layer of the mathematical reality.

-----

### Tensors

A tensor is a multi-dimensional array of numbers. Every weight in every layer of every neural network is a tensor. The input to the model is a tensor. The activations at every layer are tensors. The output before decoding is a tensor.

```
When you send a message to an AI:

Your words become tokens.
Tokens become integer indices.
Indices are looked up in an embedding tensor.
Embedding vectors pass through weight tensors
in every attention layer.
Every operation is tensor multiplication.
Every layer transforms one tensor into another.

Language enters at the beginning.
Language exits at the end.
Between those two moments:
tensors operating on tensors.
Nothing else.
```

The word “tensor” is not a metaphor for the mathematics. It is the mathematics. The system is, literally, a very large collection of tensors and the operations between them.

-----

### Manifolds

The data an AI processes — and the internal representations it builds — do not live in flat space. They live on manifolds: curved, high-dimensional geometric surfaces embedded in even higher-dimensional spaces.

```
The latent space of a language model
is a high-dimensional manifold.

Every concept, every relationship,
every piece of knowledge the model has
is a region or a point on this manifold.

Reasoning — to the extent the system reasons —
is traversal across this manifold.

The geometry of the manifold determines
what the system can reach,
what connections are available,
what transitions are possible.

The manifold is the C-axis.
It is the structural layer.
It is what the Morrison Framework™ calls
Topology( Reach( X₀, U, t ) ).
```

You cannot govern a manifold with a sentence. The manifold does not read sentences. It is a geometric object. It responds to geometric operations.

-----

### Gradients

A gradient is the direction of steepest ascent in a mathematical landscape. Training an AI is gradient descent — repeatedly computing which direction the weights need to move to reduce the loss, and moving them a small step in that direction.

```
Training loop:

  Forward pass:   input → tensors → output
  Loss computed:  how wrong was the output?
  Backward pass:  compute gradient of loss
                  with respect to every weight
  Update:         move every weight
                  a small step down the gradient

This repeats billions of times.
The weights — the tensors — are shaped
by accumulated gradient steps.

The model that emerges from training
is a configuration of numbers
that was carved by gradients
from a loss landscape
described entirely in mathematics.
```

The model was not taught values in language. It was shaped by gradients computed from mathematical loss functions. The values — to whatever extent they exist — are encoded in the geometry of the weight manifold. Not in the sentences used to describe them.

The forbidden region is excluded from the topology.

Topology is the deepest layer of the mathematical reality. It is what the framework is built from. And it is what language — operating on a different axis entirely — cannot reach.
